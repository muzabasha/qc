## Overview

This roadmap provides a structured approach to building quantum computing research capabilities, from initial publications to establishing a fully-functional Center of Excellence. Each research topic follows a 5-phase progression:

**Phase 1:** Research Publications (6-12 months)  
**Phase 2:** Seed Money & Grants (12-18 months)  
**Phase 3:** Industry Partnerships (18-24 months)  
**Phase 4:** Funded Proposals (24-36 months)  
**Phase 5:** Center of Excellence & UG Courses (36+ months)

## Topic 1: Quantum Algorithms

### 🎯 Research Focus
Development and optimization of quantum algorithms for practical applications, including Grover's search, Shor's factoring, and variational quantum algorithms (VQE, QAOA).

### Phase 1: Research Publications (Months 1-12)

#### Initial Research Areas
1. **Hybrid Quantum-Classical Algorithms**
   - Study QAOA for combinatorial optimization
   - Benchmark VQE for molecular simulation
   - Develop novel hybrid architectures

2. **Algorithm Optimization**
   - Circuit depth reduction techniques
   - Gate count minimization
   - Noise-resilient algorithm design

3. **Comparative Studies**
   - Classical vs quantum performance analysis
   - Scalability studies
   - Resource requirement estimation

#### Publication Strategy

**Target Journals:**
- IEEE Transactions on Quantum Engineering
- Quantum Science and Technology
- npj Quantum Information
- Physical Review A

**Conference Targets:**
- IEEE Quantum Week
- QIP (Quantum Information Processing)
- QTML (Quantum Techniques in Machine Learning)

**Expected Output:** 3-5 publications in first year

## Risk Mitigation

### ⚠️ Potential Challenges and Solutions

#### Challenge 1: Faculty Recruitment
**Risk:** Difficulty finding qualified quantum computing faculty

**Mitigation:**
- Hire from related fields (physics, CS, EE) and provide training
- Visiting faculty from industry/international universities
- Postdoctoral fellows as temporary faculty
- Online collaboration with remote experts

#### Challenge 2: Funding Delays
**Risk:** Grant approval delays affecting timeline

**Mitigation:**
- Apply to multiple funding sources simultaneously
- Maintain 6-12 month funding buffer
- Diversify funding (government + industry + university)
- Start with low-cost research (simulations)

#### Challenge 3: Hardware Access
**Risk:** Limited access to quantum computers

**Mitigation:**
- Use free cloud platforms (IBM Quantum)
- Academic programs (AWS, Azure, IonQ)
- Focus on simulation and algorithm development
- Partner with organizations having hardware access

#### Challenge 4: Student Interest
**Risk:** Low student enrollment initially

**Mitigation:**
- Awareness campaigns and workshops
- Industry speaker series
- Scholarships and fellowships
- Career counseling and placement support
- Start with electives before full programs

#### Challenge 5: Industry Skepticism
**Risk:** Industry hesitant to invest in quantum

**Mitigation:**
- Demonstrate clear ROI with pilot projects
- Focus on near-term NISQ applications
- Hybrid quantum-classical solutions
- Education and awareness programs
- Success stories and case studies
